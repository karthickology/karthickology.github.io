Link Building Fundamentals

Why Links Matter

Backlinks serve as votes of confidence from other websites. Google's algorithm uses these links to determine a site's authority, relevance, and trustworthiness in specific topics.

Types of Backlinks

Editorial Links

Natural links earned through valuable content. Highest quality.

DA 70+

Guest Post Links

Links acquired through contributed articles on relevant sites.

DA 40-70

Self-Created Links

Directory listings, social profiles, forum signatures.

DA 0-30

Link Building Quality Checklist

Prospecting & Research

  • Domain Authority (DA) check: Minimum DA 30+ for quality links
  • Relevance assessment: Site must be topically relevant
  • Traffic analysis: Verify site receives organic traffic
  • Spam score check: Less than 5% spam score
  • Link profile analysis: Check for toxic backlinks
  • Contact information verification

Outreach & Acquisition

  • Personalized email subject line (not generic)
  • Mention specific content from their site
  • Clear value proposition for their audience
  • Specific link placement suggestion
  • Follow-up sequence planned (3-4 emails)
  • Relationship building approach, not transactional

Post-Acquisition

  • Verify link is dofollow (unless agreed otherwise)
  • Check link is placed in content body, not footer/sidebar
  • Monitor link for at least 90 days after placement
  • Add site to monitoring list for future opportunities
  • Send thank you email and share content on social media
  • Update tracking spreadsheet with all details

Advanced Link Building Strategies

Digital PR & Newsjacking

Create data-driven studies, surveys, or research that journalists and bloggers want to cite. Monitor trending news in your industry and provide expert commentary or unique angles.

Creative White Hat

Technical SEO Opportunities

Identify unlinked brand mentions using tools like Mention or Brand24. Create helpful tools or calculators that naturally attract backlinks. Develop comprehensive resource pages that become link magnets.

Technical White Hat

Link Reclamation Process

  • Find unlinked brand mentions using Google alerts and mention tools
  • Check for broken links pointing to your competitors (opportunity to replace)
  • Identify existing links that could be improved (change anchor text)
  • Monitor lost links and attempt to regain them
  • Update old guest posts with new links to fresh content

Link Building Risk Management

Google Penalty Warning Signs

Avoid: Link farms, PBNs (Private Blog Networks), paid links without nofollow, excessive exact-match anchor text, links from irrelevant sites, and rapid unnatural link growth.

Safe Link Building Practices

Practice Safe Approach Risky Approach Google Guidelines
Anchor Text Brand + generic (80%), Partial match (15%), Exact match (5%) 70%+ exact match anchors Avoid over-optimization
Link Velocity Natural growth: 20-50 links/month for established sites 500+ links in one month Natural patterns only
Link Sources Diverse: blogs, news, educational, government Only directories or low-quality sites Quality over quantity
Reciprocal Links Occasional, natural partnerships Systematic link exchanges Keep natural and limited

Mobile-First Link Building

Mobile-Specific Strategies

With mobile-first indexing, ensure linked pages are mobile-friendly. Focus on sites with strong mobile presence. Create content formats that work well on mobile (shorter paragraphs, clear CTAs).

Mobile Link Building Checklist

  • Verify target site is mobile-friendly (Google Mobile-Friendly Test)
  • Check page load speed on mobile (<3 seconds)
  • Ensure linked content is readable on mobile (font size, spacing)
  • Prioritize sites with strong mobile traffic (SimilarWeb data)
  • Create mobile-optimized content for guest posts
  • Test link placement works on mobile view
